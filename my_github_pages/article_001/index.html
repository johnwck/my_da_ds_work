Another Use of Hierarchical Clustering



Background

1.  The department developed a new method to do a task to reduce its duration.

2.  An experiment was designed, planned and run.

3.  Data on the (a) duration, (b) hour of day, and (c) method were collected for analysis.



Result

There was no significant difference in the task duration between the control and new methods.



Next Step - The Why

1.  Hierarchical Clustering was used to analyse the data.

2.  The (a) duration, (b) hour of day, and (c) method were input variables to the analysis.



Result

1.  There were 2 distinct clusters in the dendrogram.

2.  Cluster 1 has:

  • Marginally lower hour of day
  • Significantly lower task duration
  • Higher count of new method

3.  Hypothesis: New method with lower task duration are found in the early hours of the day.



Confirmation/Disconfirmation of Hypothesis

1.  It is true that the task duration of new method is only lower between 12 am and 8 am.
