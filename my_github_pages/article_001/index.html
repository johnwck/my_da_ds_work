Another Use of Hierarchical Clustering



Background

1.  The team developed a new method to do a task to reduce its duration.

2.  An experiment was designed, planned, and run to qualify the new method.

3.  Data on the (a) duration, (b) hour of day, and (c) method were collected for analysis.



Result

The new method has marginally lower task duration than the control method.



Next Step - The Why

1.  Hierarchical Clustering was used to mine the data for insights.

2.  The (a) duration, (b) hour of day, and (c) method were used as input variables to the analysis.



Result

1.  There were 2 distinct clusters in the dendrogram.

2.  Cluster 1 has:

  • Marginally lower hour of day
  • Significantly lower task duration
  • Higher count of new method

3.  Hypothesis: The new method in the early hours of the day has lower task duration.



Confirmation/Disconfirmation of Hypothesis

1.  It is true that the task duration of the new method is only lower than the control method between 12 am and 8 am.

2.  This insight provided a direction for the team to dive into to understand this phenomenon.
