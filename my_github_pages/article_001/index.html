Another Use of Hierarchical Clustering


This is based on a real work done by the team and me. I did the data analysis for them.

Article date: 29 Oct 2023



Background

1.  The team developed a new method to do a task to reduce its duration.

2.  An experiment was designed, planned, and run to study the new method.

3.  Data on the (a) duration, (b) hour of day, and (c) method were collected for analysis.


First Result

The new method has a marginally lower task duration than the control method.




Next Step - Understand the Why

1.  Hierarchical Clustering was used to mine the data for insights.

2.  The (a) duration, (b) hour of day, and (c) method were used as input variables to the analysis.


Second Result

1.  There were 2 distinct clusters in the dendrogram.

    

2.  Comparing the input variables against the clusters, it can be seen that Cluster 1 has:

  • Marginally lower hour of day
  • Significantly lower task duration
  • Higher count of new method
    

3.  Hypothesis: The new method in the early hours of the day has lower task duration.


(Dis)Confirmation of Hypothesis

1.  Indeed the task duration of the new method is only lower than the control method between 12 am and 8 am.

    

2.  This insight gave the team a direction to dive in further to understand this phenomenon.
